Rainbow At My Window

    I peered out of my window, and was mesmerized by the beautiful rainbow.

    It was vibrant and magnificent, attracting the attention of children, little and innocent.


    It was uplifting and bright, undoubtedly a marvelous sight.

    I wish I could ride it, alas, I only could stand beside it.


    It appeared in the middle of summer, which was bit of a bummer.

    Spreading hues in the sky were its seven colors, for me they were no less than seven wonders.


    Just like how the sun and rain meet to make a beautiful rainbow,

    In our life we need to embrace moments of joy and sorrow.

The Gloomy Moon

    Sitting under the moonlit sky, I heard a muffled cry.

    The cry seemed to be coming from somewhere high,

    So I glanced above and saw the little moon cry.


    This made me very sad, because I couldn’t make the beautiful moon glad.

    I tried to soothe the moon by making it happy, but instead it got very snappy.


    When I asked the moon the cause of its despair,

    It lamented that its life is so unfair.

    It wanted to shine as bright as the sun,

    And its gentle gleam was no fun.

    .....


The Gloomy Moon....

    It had asked the sun for some of its light

    But the jealous sun had denied the request outright.

    “The sun might be the supreme light,” I told the moon,

    “But you are the one that takes away all the gloom.”

Beyond the Box Poetry Contest

(Winner 11-14yrs age group)

    It was a sight to behold, but the atmosphere was so cold.

    All around me was a bed of snow, because of which I got a frostbite on my toe.


    Nature was resplendent in all its might, when all of a sudden I saw somebody flying a kite.

    It said its winter, when friends indulge in light banter.


    The landscape was marvelous, gentle and harmless.

    The entire area was white, pristine and bright.


    It shone in the faint sunshine, I thought it to be a splatter of red wine.

    I wondered if it was in the iridescent snow spreading hues of the rainbow,

    Stomping on it I realized, it was the threatening phenomenon of red snow.
